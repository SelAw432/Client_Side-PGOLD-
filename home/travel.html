Travel Advice

Under the current restriction, everyone is to stay at home. Yoy can neither leave your home or travel. This includes internationally, unless given legal permission to do so.
If you wish to travel, you must provide evidence of negative COVID-19 test. This should be taken up to 3 days before departure or else you face the risk of not being allowed to board the plane. When returning to the UK there are some details that have to be provided. Such mandatory details includes journey and contact details. You are also required to self isolate unless you have a valid exemption. Below is a checklist that you MUST take into consideration when considering travelling.

Before you travel

  • Follow all the current rules where you live. Travel only if given legal permission to so
  • Keep up to date with current issues. Sign up for travel advice emails to know the newest developments on travel health guidance.
  • Check on any entry restrictions. These includes quarantine requirements on arrival or screening.
  • Check if you need proof of a negative COVID test
  • Check accomodation providers on safety measures put in place
  • Check up on advice of local measures put in place of there are some local restrictions
  • Check Cancellation rights
  • Try and obtain travel insurance

When youre abroad

  • Follow up any updates on travel advice for your destination
  • Prepare to obey measures to manage localised outbreaks such as border cleasures, movement restrictions or quarantine rules
  • If you test positive you are most likely to be treated locally and staying till you recover
  • Plan ahead for any delays and for any financial implications in case you need to stay longer
  • Before returning to the UK provide contact and journey details. You have to self isolate when you arrive.

When you return

  • You must follow the rules for entering the UK
  • Before returning you must provide proof of negative COVID test results at most 3 days before departure.
  • You must provide proof of passenger locator form
  • Self isolate when you enter UK.
